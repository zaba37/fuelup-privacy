1) Information We May Collect

To provide login, sync, file storage, reminders, analytics, advertising in the free version, subscriptions, and optional voice features, we may process the following categories of data:

  • Account and authentication data. If you sign in with Apple, Google, or Facebook, we may receive your unique user ID and, where permitted by the provider and you, your name, email address, and profile photo. We use this data to authenticate you and operate your account.
  • App content you create. We process the content you save in the app, such as vehicles, fuel logs, service logs, reminders, categories, settings, and similar account-linked records. This data may be stored in Firebase Realtime Database so it can sync across devices.
  • Files, attachments, and vehicle documents. If you add photos, scans, PDFs, office documents, archives, or other supported files to entries or vehicle records, the app may store local copies on your device. When cloud sync is available for your account, related metadata may be stored in Firebase Realtime Database and cloud copies, thumbnails, and related file paths may be stored in Firebase Storage.
  • Receipt scanning data. If you use AI receipt scanning, we may process receipt or invoice images, OCR or extracted text, and draft field values generated from that content in order to help populate a form for your review. If you choose to keep the receipt image as an attachment after saving, that image is treated as a regular attachment and follows the storage, sync, and retention rules that apply to attachments.
  • Attachment sync and storage metadata. To manage file syncing and storage availability, we may process technical metadata such as file names, file types, MIME types, sizes, thumbnails, sync state, storage paths, cloud URLs, deletion markers, and storage usage or quota records.
  • Purchases and subscription status. Payments are processed by Apple In-App Purchases. We do not receive or store your full card or billing details. We may receive receipt, entitlement, subscription lifecycle, renewal, expiration, and related status information from Apple and RevenueCat to unlock or restrict Premium features and to manage retention flows tied to cloud attachments.
  • Usage, diagnostics, and performance data. We use Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics to understand app usage and improve stability. This may include device model, OS version, app version, crash traces, and similar diagnostic data.
  • Advertising and tracking data. In the free version, we may use Google AdMob to show ads. AdMob may process device identifiers, advertising data, and anti-fraud signals. If you allow app tracking on iOS, advertising identifiers may be used in accordance with Apple and Google policies. Premium removes in-app ads.
  • Notifications and messaging data. If notifications are enabled, we may store push notification tokens, language code, locale identifier, app version, and related technical metadata in order to send reminder notifications and retention-related notices.
  • Voice logging data. If you use voice logging, the app may access your microphone and speech recognition permissions, create a transcript from your spoken input using iOS speech recognition services, and send the resulting transcript together with limited app context to Firebase AI using Google AI models in order to generate a draft entry for your review.
  • Permission-related device data. Depending on which features you use, the app may request access to location, camera, photo library, files, microphone, speech recognition, notifications, and app tracking settings.

4) Social Logins

If you choose to sign in with Apple, Google, or Facebook, the respective provider may share profile data as described above. We use it only to authenticate you and operate your account or session. Your use of those login providers is also subject to their own terms and privacy policies, and you can revoke access in the provider's settings.

5) Voice Logging

FuelUp offers an optional voice logging feature. If you use it, the app requests microphone and speech recognition access, captures your spoken input while the feature is active, and uses iOS speech recognition services to produce a transcript. The transcript, together with limited context such as relevant app settings or vehicle context, may then be processed by Firebase AI with a Google AI backend to generate a draft fuel-log entry for your review before saving.

We do not promise that Apple, Firebase, Google, or other third-party providers retain no data when their services are used. Their handling of data is governed by their own policies and terms.

6) AI Receipt Scanning

FuelUp may offer an optional AI receipt scanning feature. If you use it, a receipt or invoice image may be preprocessed in the app and then sent to Firebase AI with a Google AI backend in order to extract or structure information for a draft entry or form. Generated results may be incomplete or inaccurate, so you should review and correct them before saving.

If you decide to save the receipt or invoice image as part of an entry, that image may also be stored locally on your device and, where cloud sync is available for your account, synced or retained in the same way as other attachments. If you do not keep the image as an attachment, the image is still subject to processing by the third-party AI services involved in the scan flow according to their own policies.

7) Notifications
